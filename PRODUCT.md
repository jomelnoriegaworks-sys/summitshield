# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Individuals, families, homeowners, drivers, and small-business owners comparing insurance coverage. They may feel uncertain about policy language, distrust high-pressure sales tactics, or lack time to compare carriers. Their job is to understand their options, gain confidence in an independent recommendation, and book a free consultation with minimal friction.

## Product Purpose

Summit Shield Insurance is a fictional independent insurance agency presented as a production-quality portfolio case study. The marketing site explains the agency's value, establishes trust, presents five coverage categories, and converts qualified visitors into consultation bookings through a GoHighLevel form. Success means visitors quickly understand what Summit Shield does, why its independent advice is credible, and how to start.

## Brand Personality

Calm, dependable, and human. The voice is professional without sounding institutional, friendly without becoming casual, and confident without pressure. The experience should leave visitors feeling protected, informed, and comfortable taking the next step.

## Anti-references

- High-pressure lead-generation funnels with countdown timers, exaggerated urgency, or repeated popups
- Generic insurance templates built from interchangeable blue card grids and stock shield icons
- Dense policy-information pages that expose jargon before establishing relevance
- Trend-driven SaaS styling that feels technical, neon, glassy, or detached from a family-focused service
- Overanimated portfolio concepts that compromise clarity, trust, accessibility, or GoHighLevel compatibility

## Design Principles

1. Build trust before asking for information.
2. Explain choices in plain language and reveal detail progressively.
3. Keep one clear conversion path from every major decision point.
4. Use real human situations and specific proof instead of generic claims.
5. Make every interaction work cleanly on mobile and inside GoHighLevel.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, descriptive image alternatives, 44px minimum touch targets, sufficient color contrast, zoom support, and reduced-motion behavior. Do not rely on color, hover, or animation alone to communicate meaning.
