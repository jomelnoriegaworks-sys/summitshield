# Summit Shield Homepage Optimization Brief

## Objective

You are a senior UX designer, CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
specialist, and frontend engineer.

You are working on a production-quality website for a fictional
independent insurance agency called **Summit Shield Insurance**.

The website will eventually be imported into **GoHighLevel**, so
preserve all existing HTML structure, CSS architecture, responsiveness,
and GHL compatibility.

Your task is **not to redesign the website**. Your task is to simplify
it.

The homepage should answer: 1. What does Summit Shield do? 2. Why trust
Summit Shield? 3. What services are offered? 4. Why choose Summit
Shield? 5. How does the process work? 6. Can I trust them? 7. How do I
get started?

## Keep

-   Hero
-   Trust Bar
-   Problem
-   Services Overview (cards only)
-   Why Summit Shield comparison
-   3-Step Process
-   Testimonials
-   FAQ
-   Contact / Consultation
-   Footer

## Remove

-   "You're not lazy..."
-   "Walk away knowing your family is covered..."
-   Individual service detail sections
-   "Twelve years, thousands of policies..."
-   "The consultation is free..."
-   "If the call isn't useful..."
-   "Life insurance gets more expensive every birthday."

Move detailed educational content to dedicated service pages.

## Final Structure

1.  Hero
2.  Trust Bar
3.  Problem
4.  Services
5.  Why Summit Shield
6.  Process
7.  Testimonials
8.  FAQ
9.  Contact
10. Footer

## Technical Requirements

-   Preserve styling
-   Preserve responsiveness
-   Preserve animations
-   Preserve GoHighLevel compatibility
-   Remove unused CSS/JS
-   Verify navigation

## Challenge My Decisions

Do **not** blindly follow these instructions.

If you believe any removed section materially improves UX, conversions,
trust, or information architecture:

1.  Explain why.
2.  Support your recommendation with UX/CRO best practices.
3.  Propose a better alternative.
4.  Present your recommendation before making the change.

Act like a senior product designer collaborating with another
professional, not an assistant executing commands.
